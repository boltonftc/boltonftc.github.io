OnShape CAD Training

This summer series takes you from zero to confidently designing parts in OnShape. We start with the foundational OnShape Learning Center curriculum and then move into FTC-robotics-specific work — sketching, modeling, assemblies, and eventually building our full robot in CAD. Complete the lessons in order.

  • 1

    Getting Started — Introduction to CAD

    Log in to OnShape, set up your workspace, and complete two foundational modules: Parametric Feature-Based CAD and Introduction to Sketching.
  • 2

    Introduction to Part Studios

    Become familiar with the tools within OnShape's modeling environment and complete five hands-on part exercises: Control Arm, Reducer Coupling, Jackhammer Gear Cover, Funnel, and Rocket Guidance Reflector.
  • 3

    Introduction to OnShape Assemblies

    Understand how to create an Assembly with OnShape Mates. Complete three exercises: Start an Assembly, Pneumatic Cylinder, and Assembly Structure.
  • 4

    More OnShape Modules — Coming Soon

    Drawings, FTC-specific robot design, and more.

Goal: By the start of fall season, you'll be able to take your ideas, model them in CAD, and turn them into real parts — 3D printed or machined. We also plan to build our robot fully in CAD so the whole team can visualize the design.
