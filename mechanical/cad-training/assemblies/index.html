CAD Training — Lesson 3

Introduction to OnShape Assemblies

This is Lesson 3 of the Bolton FTC summer CAD training series. In this lesson you'll learn how to bring multiple parts together into an Assembly — the OnShape workspace where individual parts get connected and constrained to each other using Mates. Assemblies are how you'll model a complete robot mechanism: every motor, bracket, shaft, and gear placed in its correct position relative to everything else.

Work through the full Introduction to OnShape Assemblies course on learn.onshape.com, then complete the three exercises using the reference videos below if you get stuck.

Need help logging in or finding the course? See Lesson 1 — it walks through login and navigating to learn.onshape.com step by step.

💡 Key Idea
A Mate defines the relationship between two parts — it tells OnShape how they connect and how they can (or can't) move relative to each other. A Fastened Mate locks two parts rigidly together. A Revolute Mate allows rotation around an axis, like a hinge or axle. Choosing the right mate type is what makes a virtual assembly behave like the real thing.
✅ What to Do
  1. Open the Introduction to OnShape Assemblies course on learn.onshape.com.
  2. Work through all the lessons in order — watch the videos and complete all knowledge checks.
  3. Complete all three exercises listed below. Use the reference videos if you get stuck.
  4. Mark the course complete before moving on.
💜 Hint
When adding a mate, OnShape highlights valid mate connectors as you hover over parts. If a part is flying off to an unexpected location, check that you selected the right connector on each part — it's easy to accidentally pick a face instead of an axis. Undo (Ctrl+Z) and try again.
