CAD Training — Lesson 5

Parallel Plate Drivetrain

This is Lesson 5 — the first FTC-specific lesson in the series. You'll build a parallel plate drivetrain in OnShape by following a tutorial from Renert Robotics. Parallel plate drivetrains are used by many competitive FTC teams and are a solid foundation for designing your own robot base.

This lesson is more challenging than the previous ones. You'll create new parts from scratch, modify existing parts, and work through a more complex assembly. That's the point — this is where skills from the earlier lessons get applied to something real. Don't expect it to go perfectly on the first try.

Example parallel plate drivetrain — this is what you'll be building.

The Tutorial

✅ What to Do
  1. Create a new OnShape document in your student training folder. (See Lesson 1 if you need a reminder on how to find that folder.)
  2. Open the tutorial video below in a separate tab or window.
  3. Work through the tutorial: watch a section, then switch to OnShape and do that part, then go back and watch the next section. Don't try to watch the whole thing first.
  4. Build the full parallel plate drivetrain. Getting stuck and working through it is part of the exercise.

Where This is Heading
