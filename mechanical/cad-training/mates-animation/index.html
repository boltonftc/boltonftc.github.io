CAD Training — Lesson 6

Mates and Animation — Crank-Slider Mechanism

This is Lesson 6. You'll build a complete crank-slider mechanism from scratch — modeling all five parts in a single Part Studio, assembling them with the correct mate types, and then animating the assembly so the crank drives the slider.

The mechanism has five parts: a rail, a slider, a crank, a connecting rod, and a pivot. You'll model each one using tools you've seen before — center rectangles, offsets, slots, and extrusions — then move into the assembly environment to connect them. The mates you'll apply are a slider mate for linear motion and revolute mates at each pivot point. When it's done, you can run the animation on the primary revolute mate and watch the whole thing move.

The finished crank-slider mechanism animating in OnShape.

Why This Matters for FTC

FTC robots are full of mechanisms that move — intakes that fold out, arms that rotate, shooters that spin, and linkages that transfer motion from a motor to an end effector. Before you build any of those in real life, you can model them in CAD and verify that the motion actually works the way you intend.

Animated mates let you check that parts don't collide during travel, that a crank arm clears the frame at all angles, or that a slider reaches the positions it needs to. Catching those problems in CAD takes minutes. Catching them after you've cut and drilled aluminum takes much longer. The crank-slider in this lesson is a simple example of the same technique you'll use to validate real robot mechanisms.

💡 Key Idea
A slider mate constrains a part to move along a single axis — like a drawer or a linear slide. A revolute mate constrains rotation around a single axis — like a hinge or an axle. Combining them is how you model mechanisms that convert one type of motion into another, which is exactly what motors on a robot do.

Tutorial

✅ What to Do
  1. Create a new OnShape document in your student training folder. (See Lesson 1 if you need a reminder.)
  2. Open the tutorial video below in a separate tab.
  3. Work through it section by section — watch a portion, then switch to OnShape and build that part before continuing.
  4. Model all five parts in the Part Studio, then assemble and mate them.
  5. Animate the primary revolute mate on a loop and confirm the slider moves correctly.
