CAD Training — Lesson 4

Inspection and Repair Tools

This is Lesson 4 of the Bolton FTC summer CAD training series — and the last of the foundational OnShape Learning Center modules. This lesson teaches you how to find and fix problems in your models using OnShape's built-in diagnostic tools. You'll learn to identify errors in sketch geometry, track down missing references, and resolve the kind of broken-model warnings that will eventually show up in any real design project.

Work through the full Inspection and Repair Tools course on learn.onshape.com.

Need help logging in or finding the course? See Lesson 1 — it walks through login and navigating to learn.onshape.com step by step.

💡 Key Idea
A missing reference happens when a feature was built on top of geometry that no longer exists — for example, a sketch was deleted that an Extrude depended on. OnShape marks these with a warning icon in the Feature List. The Inspection and Repair tools let you see exactly what broke and reattach features to new geometry without rebuilding from scratch.
✅ What to Do
  1. Open the Inspection and Repair Tools course on learn.onshape.com.
  2. Work through all the lessons in order — watch the videos and complete all knowledge checks.
  3. Mark the course complete when done.
💜 Hint
These tools will feel most useful once you've been modeling for a while and start running into real errors. Even if nothing breaks during the lesson exercises, pay attention — knowing how to diagnose a broken model quickly is a skill that separates beginners from confident designers.
