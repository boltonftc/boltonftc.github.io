CAD Training

Getting Started with OnShape

This is the first of several modules in our summer CAD training series. We'll be working through the excellent OnShape Learning Center curriculum and then moving into FTC-robotics-specific design work. The goal: by the time fall season starts, you'll be able to take your ideas, model them in CAD, and turn them into real parts — 3D printed or machined. We also plan to build our robot fully in CAD so the whole team can see and understand the design.

Watch the intro video below — it walks you through logging in to OnShape, where to save your lesson work, and how to navigate to learn.onshape.com to start the Introduction to CAD learning path.

This Session: Two Modules

Head to the Introduction to CAD learning path on learn.onshape.com and complete the following two modules in order.

Module 1 Introduction to Parametric Feature-Based CAD

This module explains what parametric, feature-based CAD actually means — the core idea behind how OnShape (and professional engineering software) works. You'll learn why changing one dimension can update an entire model automatically, and why that matters when you're designing robot parts that need to fit together precisely.

✅ What to Do
  1. Open the Introduction to CAD learning path.
  2. Select "Introduction to Parametric Feature-Based CAD".
  3. Work through all the lessons in the module — watch the videos and complete the knowledge checks.
  4. Mark the module complete before moving on.
💡 Key Idea
Parametric means your model is driven by numbers (parameters). Change a single dimension and the whole part updates — no redrawing required. This is how professional engineers and FTC teams iterate quickly on designs.
Module 2 Introduction to Sketching

Sketching is the foundation of all CAD work — almost every 3D part starts with a 2D sketch. This module teaches you how to draw constrained sketches in OnShape: lines, circles, arcs, dimensions, and the geometric constraints that lock your sketch into the right shape.

✅ What to Do
  1. From the same learning path, select "Introduction to Sketching".
  2. Work through the module lessons and complete all exercises inside OnShape.
  3. If you get stuck on the Basic or Intermediate Sketching exercises, watch the reference videos below — they show a complete walkthrough of each exercise.
💡 Key Idea
A sketch is fully constrained when every line and point is locked in place by dimensions or geometric rules (parallel, perpendicular, tangent, etc.). OnShape turns the sketch black when it's fully constrained. Blue means something is still floating — that's a sign to add more constraints before you move on.
💜 Hint
Don't skip the constraints. It's tempting to just drag things into place, but a sketch without proper constraints will shift when you change a dimension later. Lock everything down the right way from the start.
