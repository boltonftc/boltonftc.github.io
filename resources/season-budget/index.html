💡 Recommended Additional Budget Items

Items not currently in the budget that are commonly needed over the course of a full FTC season.

Limelight 3A — One Per Robot

Currently only 1 spare for 2 teams. AprilTag detection and vision is core to autonomous. Each robot needs its own.

~$189 × 2 = $378

Ferrite Choke Bulk Pack

Snap-on ferrite chokes for every signal cable (encoders, servos, sensors). Cheap, highly effective ESD protection.

~$15–25

3D Printing Filament (PETG, 2kg)

Custom sensor mounts, intake guides, cable routing clips, and field element practice pieces. You'll go through it.

~$40–60

Wire & Electrical Consumables

Bulk heat shrink kit, zip tie assortment, velcro straps, electrical tape, spare JST connectors. Burns through fast over a season.

~$50–75

Anderson Powerpole Kit + Crimper

Covers the "TBD" wiring line — makes clean, custom-length power cables. A good crimper pays for itself in saved time.

~$40 tool + $20 connectors

Spare REV Servo Extension Cables (25-pack)

Most frequently damaged/lost cable at competition. Cheap to stock, expensive to be without.

~$30–40

Team Shirts / Uniforms

Judges and alliance partners notice a cohesive team. Required at most events to look "put together." Plan for 10–15 shirts.

~$200–350 (screen print)

Pit Infrastructure

Folding table, team banner/backdrop, rolling tool bag or case. First-year teams often arrive at their first event without these.

~$150–300

Dedicated Programming Laptop (Per Team)

Students shouldn't be fighting over a laptop at competition. A refurbished ThinkPad or similar runs Android Studio fine.

~$250–400 refurb

Backup Driver Station Device

You have 1 spare Driver Hub for 2 teams. A DS failure during quals is catastrophic. A second backup is worth the insurance.

~$275 (REV Driver Hub)
